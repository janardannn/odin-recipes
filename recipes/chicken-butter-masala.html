Chicken Butter Masala

Butter chicken, traditionally known as murgh makhani, an Indian dish originating in Delhi, which is a type of curry made from chicken with a spiced tomato and butter (makhan) sauce. Its sauce is known for its rich texture.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ pound chicken
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashews
  • 1 teaspoon ground red chiles
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ½ cup half-and-half
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Step 1 Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat; fry chicken in batches until golden, about 5 minutes. Transfer fried chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain, retaining vegetable oil in skillet.
  2. Step 2 Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat; cook and stir onion until golden brown, about 10 minutes. Add ginger paste and garlic paste. Continue to cook until fragrant, about 1 minute more. Stir cashews, ground red chiles, cumin, coriander, and garam masala into the onion mixture. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
  3. Step 3 Stir tomato sauce, half-and-half, milk, sugar, and salt into spice mixture; simmer until thickened, about 5 minutes. Reduce heat to low. Add fried chicken and simmer until heated through, about 5 minutes more.
